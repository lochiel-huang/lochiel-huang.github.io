CASE 01

AI-powered Financial Compliance AuditorAI 財務合規審查系統

Role: AI Product Manager · Personal prototype · In development (2026)角色：AI 產品經理 · 個人原型 · 開發中（2026）

The problem. Even in mid-sized financial and property-management firms, compliance review still runs on paper. Vouchers, purchase requisitions, invoices, bank slips, and approval forms are physically stapled together, cross-checked by eye, and annotated with sticky notes. Traditional OCR fails on stamped or handwritten regions; account codes are inconsistent across sources; split reimbursements break rule-based reconciliation engines entirely.

問題所在。即使在中型金融和物業管理公司，合規審查仍然跑在紙本上。憑證、請購單、發票、銀行單據和審批表被實體釘在一起，靠眼睛交叉核對，然後貼上便利貼備注。傳統 OCR 在加蓋印章或手寫區域會失效；會計科目代碼在不同來源之間不一致；差額分攤的報銷直接讓規則引擎報錯。

Design設計思路

A three-stage agent workflow: (1) multi-modal parsing extracts fields and stamps them with confidence scores; (2) a fuzzy-matching layer combines semantic embedding search with a deterministic Python solver for split-and-merge arithmetic; (3) a compliance-review agent generates virtual sticky notes — machine-authored annotations waiting for a one-click human confirmation.

三階段 agent 工作流：(1) 多模態解析提取欄位並標記置信分數；(2) 模糊匹配層結合語義嵌入搜索和確定性 Python 求解器處理差額分攤的計算；(3) 合規審查 agent 生成虛擬便利貼——機器撰寫的標注，等待一鍵人工確認。

Why this shape為什麼這樣設計
  • Anti-hallucination on numbers. All numeric matching runs through a deterministic solver; the LLM only handles language and layout.
  • 數字防幻覺。所有數字匹配通過確定性求解器處理；LLM 只負責語言和版面。
  • Human-in-the-loop by default. Confidence below threshold triggers a highlighted review, never a silent pass.
  • 預設人工在環。置信度低於閾值會觸發高亮複核，永遠不會靜默通過。
Where it came from靈感來源

The concept crystallised during my compliance internship at Huafu International Financial Holdings and my accounting internship at Sheraton Shanghai Jiading. I kept noticing the same shape of problem: the auditor's real work isn't judgement — it's the mechanical assembly of the evidence needed for judgement.

這個概念在華富國際金融控股的合規實習和喜來登上海嘉定酒店的財務實習中逐漸成形。我一直注意到同樣形狀的問題：審計師真正的工作不是判斷本身——而是為判斷收集證據的那個機械過程。

CASE 02

Field Observation Report: Social Interaction Patterns田野觀察報告：社交互動行為分析

Role: Observer & Analyst · Personal research · Documented (June 2026)角色：觀察者與分析者 · 個人研究 · 已記錄（2026年6月）

The setting. A late-night social venue. A stranger initiated contact and, over two hours, deployed a sequence of persuasion tactics: a purity narrative, appeals to feminist principles, criticism of materialistic customs, and a physically escalating request near departure. My interest was not in the individual encounter but in the structure: how consistently the tactics escalated, how the earlier statements retroactively looked like scaffolding for the later ones.

場景。一個深夜社交場所。一名陌生人主動搭訕，在兩小時內部署了一系列說服策略：純愛敘事、訴諸女權主義、批評物質化習俗，以及在離開前的肢體升級請求。我感興趣的不是這次個別相遇，而是它的結構：策略升級的一致性，以及前面的話語事後看起來像是為後來做的鋪墊。

Method方法

Following the encounter, I wrote up the sequence as a structured behavioural log: numbered event codes, timestamps, verbal and non-verbal moves, and a clear distinction between observed behaviour and observer inference.

相遇結束後，我把整個過程整理成結構化行為記錄：編號的事件代碼、時間戳、語言和非語言動作，以及對觀察行為和觀察者推斷的明確區分。

Why this counts as work為什麼這算是作品
  • Discomfort as data. The impulse to react emotionally is real; the value is in converting it into something structured that someone else could read and interrogate.
  • 不適感作為數據。情緒反應的衝動是真實的；價值在於把它轉化成別人可以閱讀和質疑的結構化內容。
  • Method transfer. The same taxonomy-and-annotation reflex is what I use in compliance work. The medium is different; the discipline is identical.
  • 方法遷移。這套分類和標注的反射動作，正是我在合規工作中使用的。媒介不同，紀律相同。
